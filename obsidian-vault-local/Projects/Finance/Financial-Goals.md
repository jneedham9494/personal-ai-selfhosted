## Key Financial Decisions

### 1. Credit Card Strategy

**Current Problem:**
- Paying full balance AFTER statement date
- High balance gets reported before payment
- Credit utilization appears high (hurts score)

**Solution (Week 1):**
- Find statement close date
- Pay balance 2-3 days BEFORE statement closes
- Reported balance = $0 or very low
- Credit utilization drops to <10%
- Score improves 20-50 points in 2-3 months

**Backup Card:**

- Apply for 2nd card (Chase Sapphire for travel recommended)
- Benefits: Travel backup, higher total credit limit

---

### 2. Tax-Efficient Investing

**Your Concern:** "Nervous about tax implications"

**Reality Check:**
- NOT INVESTING = GUARANTEED 3-4% LOSS to inflation
- INVESTING = POTENTIAL 7-10% GAIN minus taxes (still way ahead)

**Tax Math Example:**
- $100k in cash @ 0%: Value in 5 years = $86,000 (after inflation)
- $100k in index funds @ 8%: Value in 5 years = $146,933
- Taxes on gains (15% long-term): -$7,040
- After-tax value: $139,893
- **Net difference: $53,893 MORE by investing**

**Tax Strategies:**
1. Hold >1 year (15-20% tax vs 37% income tax)
2. Tax-loss harvesting
3. Index funds (lower turnover)
4. Roth IRA (tax-free growth forever)

---

### 3. House Down Payment Strategy

**Timeline:** ~5 years (2030 purchase)

**Rule of Thumb:**
- Money needed in <3 years: High-yield savings (4-5%)
- Money needed in 3-5 years: 50/50 stocks and bonds
- Money needed in 5+ years: 80-100% stocks

**Example Strategy (Assume $800k house):**
- 20% down payment: $160k needed
- Already have: $100k cash
- Still need: $60k (= $1,000/month over 5 years)
- Allocation:
  - $60k: High-yield savings (guaranteed for house)
  - $40k: Index funds (5-year growth)
  - Monthly surplus: $1,000/month → house fund
  - Remaining surplus → index funds (long-term)
