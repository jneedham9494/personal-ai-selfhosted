# 🍽️ Fitness Meal Plan & Shopping Lists

## Overview
**Purpose:** Support Q4 2025 Fitness Goal with practical meal planning
**Budget:** ~$150-200/week for 1 person
**Prep Time:** 2-3 hours on Sunday
**Approach:** High protein, whole foods, easy meal prep
**Your Activities:** Swimming, cycling to/from work, sauna recovery

## 📊 Nutrition Targets (Daily)

**Macros (Endurance-focused for swimming/cycling):**
- Calories: 2,400-2,800 (higher for cardio activities)
- Protein: 160-180g (muscle recovery from swimming)
- Carbs: 250-300g (HIGHER for endurance - fuel for cycling + swimming)
- Fats: 70-90g (hormone health, sustained energy)
- Fiber: 30-40g
- Water: 4+ liters (CRITICAL with swimming + sauna)
- Electrolytes: Sodium, potassium, magnesium (sauna depletes these)

## 💧 Hydration & Electrolyte Strategy

**Critical for Swimmers + Sauna Users:**

**Daily Hydration:**
- Morning: 16oz water upon waking
- Pre-swim: 16oz water 30 min before
- During swim: Not needed (short sessions)
- Post-swim: 20-24oz water with electrolytes
- Pre-sauna: 8-16oz water
- Post-sauna: 16-20oz water with salt/electrolytes
- Evening: Sip water throughout

**Electrolyte Sources:**
- LMNT packets (Amazon) - add to water post-sauna
- Himalayan sea salt (pinch in morning water)
- Coconut water (natural electrolytes)
- Bananas (potassium)
- Dates (potassium, quick energy)

**Sauna Recovery:**
- Post-sauna smoothie with protein + banana
- Or: Coconut water + protein shake
- Replace what you sweat out!

## 🚴‍♂️🏊‍♂️ Activity-Specific Nutrition

### Cycling Commute (To/From Work)

**Morning Ride (TO work):**
- Eat breakfast BEFORE riding
- Keep it light if short ride (<30 min)
- Banana + PB if longer ride (30-60 min)
- Water bottle for ride

**Evening Ride (FROM work):**
- Small snack before leaving work (avoid bonking)
- Apple, protein bar, or handful of nuts
- Post-ride: Full dinner within 30-60 min
- Rehydrate immediately

**Portable Snacks (keep at work):**
- Protein bars (Kirkland from Costco)
- Almonds/trail mix
- Apples
- Rice cakes with almond butter packets

### Swimming Workouts

**Pre-Swim (30-60 min before):**
- Light, easily digestible carbs
- Banana + honey
- Or: Rice cakes + jam
- Avoid heavy fats/protein (slows digestion)

**Post-Swim (within 30 min - CRITICAL):**
- Protein shake (fast-absorbing)
- Add carbs: banana, dates, or honey
- Electrolytes (LMNT packet or coconut water)
- Full meal within 1-2 hours

**Swimming Days = Higher Carbs:**
- Add extra rice, potatoes, or oats
- Swimming depletes glycogen fast
- Carb up before, refuel after

### Sauna Protocol (Post-Workout)

**Before Sauna:**
- Already hydrated from workout
- Maybe 8oz water if thirsty

**During Sauna (15-20 min):**
- Don't drink (short session)
- If longer (30+ min), sip water

**Immediately After:**
- 16-20oz water with electrolytes
- Within 15 min: Light snack (banana, dates)
- Within 30-60 min: Full meal with protein + carbs

### 📦 **Amazon Supplements**

**Essential Stack**

**1. Protein Powder**
- **Recommendation:** Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard Whey
- **Flavor:** Double Rich Chocolate or Vanilla Ice Cream
- **Why:** Best taste/mixability, 24g protein per scoop
- **Usage:** 1-2 scoops daily (post-workout + breakfast)
- **Link:** Search "ON Gold Standard Whey 5lb"
- **Price:** ~$60 for 5 lbs (73 servings)

**Alternative (Budget):**
- Bulk Supplements Whey Protein Isolate (unflavored, mix with anything)

**2. Creatine Monohydrate**
- **Recommendation:** Optimum Nutrition Micronized Creatine
- **Dosage:** 5g daily (1 tsp)
- **Why:** Most researched supplement, improves strength/muscle
- **Usage:** Mix with protein shake or water
- **Price:** ~$20 for 120 servings
- **Note:** Most effective, cheapest supplement

**3. Electrolytes (CRITICAL for Swimmer + Sauna User)**
- **Recommendation:** LMNT Electrolyte Drink Mix
- **Dosage:** 1 packet post-swim or post-sauna
- **Why:** Replenish sodium, potassium, magnesium lost in sweat
- **Flavors:** Citrus Salt, Watermelon Salt, Raspberry Salt
- **Usage:** Mix with 16-20oz water after swim/sauna
- **Price:** ~$45 for 30 packets ($1.50/serving)
- **Link:** Search "LMNT Electrolytes" on Amazon

**Alternative (Budget):**
- Liquid IV Hydration Multiplier (~$25 for 16 sticks)
- DIY: 1/4 tsp salt + 1/4 tsp NoSalt (potassium) + water + lemon
- Coconut water (natural electrolytes, get at TJ's)

**4. Fish Oil (Omega-3)**
- **Recommendation:** Nordic Naturals Ultimate Omega
- **Dosage:** 2 softgels daily (with meal)
- **Why:** Heart health, joint recovery, inflammation (great for swimmers!)
- **Price:** ~$35 for 60-day supply

**Alternative (Budget):**
- Costco/Kirkland Omega-3 (excellent quality, better price)

**5. Vitamin D3 + K2**
- **Recommendation:** Sports Research Vitamin D3+K2
- **Dosage:** 1 softgel daily (5000 IU)
- **Why:** Most people deficient, bone health, immunity
- **Price:** ~$20 for 60-day supply

**5. Multivitamin (Optional)**
- **Recommendation:** Optimum Nutrition Opti-Men (or Opti-Women)
- **Dosage:** 3 tablets daily
- **Why:** Fill nutritional gaps
- **Price:** ~$25 for 90-day supply

**Alternative:** Get most nutrients from whole foods (prioritize this)

**6. Pre-Workout (Optional)**
- **Recommendation:** Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard Pre-Workout
- **Dosage:** 1 scoop before workout
- **Why:** Energy boost, focus (contains caffeine)
- **Price:** ~$30 for 30 servings
- **Note:** Only if you workout early AM or need energy boost

**Alternative:** Black coffee (free if you already drink it!)

**7. Casein Protein (Optional - Night)**
- **Recommendation:** Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard Casein
- **Usage:** Before bed (slow-digesting protein)
- **Why:** Prevents muscle breakdown overnight
- **Price:** ~$55 for 4 lbs
- **Note:** Only if you struggle hitting protein targets

## 🍳 Meal Prep Protocol (Sunday Routine)

### Time: 2-3 hours
### Containers: 7-10 meal prep containers (get at Costco/Amazon)

**Step-by-Step:**

**1. Start Carbs (10 min setup)**
- Rice cooker: Brown rice (4 cups dry)
- Oven: Sweet potatoes (wrap in foil, 400°F, 45-60 min)
- Stovetop: Quinoa (follow package)

**2. Proteins (15 min setup, 20-30 min cook)**
- Oven method (easiest):
  - Sheet pan 1: 4-5 chicken breasts, seasoned (375°F, 25 min)
  - Sheet pan 2: Salmon fillets, lemon + seasoning (400°F, 15 min)
- Stovetop: Ground turkey (2 lbs) in skillet with taco seasoning

**3. Vegetables (while proteins cook)**
- Roast broccoli/Brussels sprouts (400°F, 20-25 min)
- Steam green beans (10 min)
- Chop salad ingredients (store separately to prevent sogginess)

**4. Cool & Portion (20 min)**
- Let everything cool 10 minutes
- Portion into containers:
  - Lunch containers: Protein (6-8oz) + Carb (1 cup) + Veggies
  - Dinner containers: Same ratio
  - Snack prep: Hard boil eggs (6-8), portion nuts

**5. Label & Store**
- Write day on each container
- Fridge: Next 3-4 days
- Freezer: Days 5-7 (thaw night before)

**Pro Tips:**
- Cook similar temps together
- Use foil/parchment for easy cleanup
- Invest in good containers (leak-proof, microwave-safe)
- Prep vegetables last (stay freshest)

## 💡 Quick Wins & Hacks

**When Short on Time:**
- Costco rotisserie chicken (shred for meals)
- TJ's frozen grilled chicken strips (pre-cooked)
- Canned tuna/salmon (quick protein)
- Microwaveable rice packets
- Pre-washed salads

**Budget Hacks:**
- Eggs are cheapest protein source
- Frozen vegetables = same nutrition, cheaper
- Buy proteins on sale, freeze portions
- Make own trail mix (Costco nuts + dried fruit)

**Travel/On-the-Go:**
- Protein shaker bottle (always)
- Protein bars in car/bag
- Apples + nut butter packets
- Beef jerky (high protein snack)

**Eating Out (Stay on Track):**
- Chipotle: Bowl, double protein, light rice
- Subway: Double meat, wheat bread
- Any restaurant: "Can I get extra chicken?"
- Skip the free bread/chips
