## Daily Structure

### Ideal Day Template

**Morning Routine (6:00-9:00 AM)**
- [ ] Wake up at consistent time
- [ ] Hydrate (water)
- [ ] Morning movement (stretch, yoga, or walk)
- [ ] Meditation or journaling (10-15 min)
- [ ] Healthy breakfast
- [ ] Review day's priorities
- [ ] Most important task (MIT) - deep work

**Midday (9:00 AM-12:00 PM)**
- [ ] Deep work block #1
- [ ] Quick break and movement
- [ ] Deep work block #2
- [ ] Check messages/email (time-boxed)

**Afternoon (12:00-5:00 PM)**
- [ ] Lunch and proper break
- [ ] Learning time or lighter tasks
- [ ] Meetings/collaboration time
- [ ] Exercise or physical activity
- [ ] Administrative tasks

**Evening (5:00-10:00 PM)**
- [ ] Wind down from work
- [ ] Dinner (mindful eating)
- [ ] Personal time (hobbies, social, rest)
- [ ] Evening routine prep
- [ ] Plan tomorrow (5 min)
- [ ] No screens 1 hour before bed
- [ ] Consistent bedtime

**Weekly Structure**
- **Monday:** Planning week, energy high
- **Tuesday-Thursday:** Deep work days
- **Friday:** Wrap up, review week, lighter work
- **Saturday:** Personal projects, hobbies, social
- **Sunday:** Rest, prep, reflection

## Decision-Making Framework

### When Evaluating Opportunities (Projects, Commitments)

**The Hell Yeah or No Test:**
- If it's not a "Hell Yeah!", it's a "No"
- Only commit to things that excite you

**The Time Cost Analysis:**
- What will I have to say no to?
- Is this aligned with my goals?
- Will future-me thank present-me?

**The Energy Audit:**
- Does this give or drain energy?
- Is this in my zone of genius?
- Can someone else do this better?
