5. Fold sides in, roll tightly

**Dinner** (Fresh - Cook tonight, ~30 min)
- **Stir-Fry Beef & Vegetables**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 sirloin steak (10-12 oz), sliced thin against the grain
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup snap peas
- 1 cup shredded carrots (or 2 carrots, julienned)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced (or 1 tsp ground ginger)
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 cup brown rice (use leftover from meal prep or cook fresh)
- Optional: 1 tbsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water for thicker sauce

**Instructions:**
1. **Prep ingredients**: Slice steak thin, chop all vegetables
2. **Cook rice** if you don't have leftover (or microwave leftover rice)
3. Heat wok or large skillet over high heat
4. Add 1 tbsp oil, swirl to coat pan
5. Add steak slices in single layer, don't move for 1-2 min (let it sear)
6. Stir-fry steak 2-3 more minutes until browned, remove to plate
7. Add bell peppers and carrots to pan, stir-fry 3 minutes
8. Add snap peas, garlic, ginger, stir-fry 2 minutes
9. Return beef to pan
10. Add soy sauce, toss everything together 1 minute
11. Optional: Add cornstarch slurry, cook 30 seconds until thickened
12. Serve over rice

**Snacks**
- Greek yogurt with granola

## RECIPE 1: Turkey Chili (Makes 6 servings, ~30-40 min active cooking)

**What You Need:**
- 2 lbs ground turkey (two 1-lb packages)
- 1 yellow onion, diced
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- 3-4 cloves garlic, minced (or 1 tbsp jarred minced garlic)
- 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 2 cans (15 oz each) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
- 2 cans (15 oz each) diced tomatoes (with juice)
- 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
- 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- 3 tbsp chili powder
- 2 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Prep ingredients** (5 min):
   - Dice onion into small pieces
   - Dice bell pepper into small pieces
   - Mince garlic (or use jarred)
   - Open all cans, drain beans

2. **Sauté aromatics** (5 min):
   - Heat large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat
   - Add 2 tbsp olive oil
   - Add diced onion and bell pepper
   - Cook, stirring occasionally, until onion is translucent (about 4-5 min)
   - Add minced garlic, cook 30 seconds until fragrant

3. **Brown turkey** (8-10 min):
   - Add both packages of ground turkey to pot
   - Break up meat with wooden spoon
   - Cook, stirring and breaking up chunks, until no pink remains
   - Meat should be browned and crumbly

4. **Add remaining ingredients** (5 min):
   - Add drained black beans and kidney beans
   - Add both cans of diced tomatoes (with juice)
   - Add tomato paste
   - Add chicken broth
   - Add chili powder, cumin, paprika
   - Add 1 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper
   - Stir well to combine everything

5. **Simmer** (30 min):
   - Bring mixture to a boil
   - Reduce heat to low
   - Cover pot, leaving lid slightly ajar
   - Simmer for 30 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes
   - **During this time, start Recipe 2 (Chicken) and Recipe 3 (Rice)**

6. **Taste and adjust** (2 min):
   - Taste chili
   - Add more salt/pepper if needed
   - Add more chili powder if you want it spicier
   - If too thick, add 1/2 cup water

7. **Cool and portion** (10 min):
   - Let chili cool for 10 minutes
   - Portion into 4 containers (about 1.5 cups each)
   - Save remaining 2 servings in a larger container for later in week
   - Label containers: "Turkey Chili - Mon/Tue/Wed/Thu"

## RECIPE 2: Grilled Chicken Breasts (Makes 5 servings, ~30 min total)

**What You Need:**
- 6 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Preheat oven** (while chili simmers):
   - Set oven to 400°F
   - Line a large baking sheet with aluminum foil for easy cleanup
   - Lightly spray or brush foil with oil

2. **Prep chicken** (5 min):
   - Pat chicken breasts dry with paper towels (important for browning!)
   - If breasts are very thick (over 1 inch), pound them to even thickness with a meat mallet or heavy pan
   - Place chicken on prepared baking sheet

3. **Season chicken** (3 min):
   - Drizzle olive oil over all chicken breasts
   - In small bowl, mix: garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, pepper
   - Sprinkle seasoning mix evenly over both sides of chicken
   - Rub seasoning in with your hands

4. **Bake** (22-25 min):
   - Place baking sheet in preheated oven
   - Bake for 22-25 minutes
   - Chicken is done when internal temp reaches 165°F (use meat thermometer)
   - Or: cut into thickest part - juices should run clear, no pink inside

5. **Rest** (5 min):
   - Remove from oven
   - Let chicken rest on baking sheet for 5 minutes (keeps it juicy!)
   - **During this time, check on chili and rice**

6. **Slice** (5 min):
   - Transfer chicken to cutting board
   - Slice each breast into strips (about 1/2 inch thick)
   - You'll use 5 breasts for meal prep bowls
   - Save 1 breast for a different meal or extra servings

## RECIPE 3: Brown Rice (Makes 6 cups cooked, ~45 min mostly hands-off)

**What You Need:**
- 2 cups dry brown rice
- 4 cups water (or low-sodium chicken broth for more flavor)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp butter or olive oil (optional, makes rice fluffier)

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

**OPTION A: Rice Cooker (Easiest)**
1. Rinse rice in fine-mesh strainer under cold water for 30 seconds
2. Add rice to rice cooker
3. Add water (follow rice cooker's ratio - usually 1:2 for brown rice)
4. Add salt
5. Press "Cook" or "Brown Rice" button
6. Wait 45-50 minutes until cooker switches to "Warm"
7. Let sit 5 minutes, then fluff with fork

**OPTION B: Stovetop**
1. **Rinse rice** (1 min):
   - Place rice in fine-mesh strainer
   - Rinse under cold water for 30 seconds (removes excess starch)

2. **Bring to boil** (5 min):
   - Add rice to medium pot
   - Add 4 cups water
   - Add 1 tsp salt and butter/oil (optional)
   - Bring to a boil over high heat

3. **Simmer** (40-45 min):
   - Once boiling, reduce heat to low
   - Cover pot with tight-fitting lid
   - DO NOT lift lid or stir during cooking!
   - Simmer for 40-45 minutes

4. **Check doneness** (2 min):
   - After 40 min, lift lid and check
   - Rice should be tender and water absorbed
   - If water remains, cook 5 more minutes uncovered

5. **Rest and fluff** (5 min):
   - Remove from heat
   - Let sit covered for 5 minutes
   - Fluff with fork

## RECIPE 4: Steamed Broccoli (Makes 5 cups, ~10 min)

**What You Need:**
- 3 heads broccoli (or 2 lbs broccoli florets)
- Water for steaming
- Salt (optional)

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Prep broccoli** (5 min):
   - Wash broccoli under cold water
   - Cut florets from stems (bite-sized pieces, about 1-2 inches)
   - Peel stems and cut into coins (optional - stems are edible!)
   - You should have about 5-6 cups raw florets

2. **Steam** (5-8 min):

   **OPTION A: Steamer Basket**
   - Fill pot with 1 inch of water
   - Place steamer basket in pot
   - Bring water to boil
   - Add broccoli to basket
   - Cover and steam 5-7 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp
   - Test with fork - should pierce easily but not mushy

   **OPTION B: Microwave**
   - Place broccoli in microwave-safe bowl
   - Add 2 tbsp water
   - Cover with microwave-safe plate or plastic wrap (leave small vent)
   - Microwave on high for 4-5 minutes
   - Check tenderness, microwave 1-2 more minutes if needed

3. **Cool** (5 min):
   - Drain broccoli
   - Spread on plate or baking sheet to cool faster
   - Pat dry with paper towel if very wet

## 🎯 Meal Prep Tips for Week 1

**Before Shopping:**
1. Check your pantry - you may already have: rice, oats, spices, oils, protein powder
2. Adjust quantities if needed
3. Total grocery cost estimate: $90-110 for the week

**At the Store:**
1. Shop perimeter first (produce, meat, dairy)
2. Get shelf-stable items last
3. Check for sales on chicken/turkey
4. Don't overbuy - this list is sized for Week 1 only

**When Cooking (Friday Night):**
1. Start rice cooker first (longest cook time, 45 min)
2. Start chili next (needs 30 min simmer time)
3. While chili simmers, prep and bake chicken (25 min)
4. Steam broccoli while chicken rests (8 min)
5. Let everything cool 5-10 min before portioning
6. Assembly line the bowls - fastest method

**Equipment You'll Need:**
- Large pot or Dutch oven (for chili)
- Large baking sheet (for chicken)
- Rice cooker OR medium pot with lid (for rice)
- Steamer basket OR microwave-safe bowl (for broccoli)
- 5 meal prep containers (for chicken bowls)
- 4 meal prep containers (for chili)
- Cutting board and sharp knife
- Wooden spoon, tongs, measuring cups/spoons

**Storage:**
- Glass containers best for reheating (microwave-safe)
- Plastic containers work fine too (BPA-free)
- Label each container with meal name + day (use masking tape + marker)
- Chili lasts 5 days in fridge, 3 months in freezer
- Chicken bowls last 4 days max in fridge - eat Mon-Thu
- Keep teriyaki sauce separate in small container or add when eating

**Reheating:**
- Microwave chicken bowls: 2-3 minutes, stir halfway
- Microwave chili: 2 minutes, stir, then 1 more minute
- Add teriyaki sauce to chicken bowls after reheating
- Can eat chicken bowls cold if you prefer

**Type 3 Reminder:**
- Week 1 meals don't need to be Instagram-perfect
- Function > aesthetics
- Eating = fueling your workouts
- Simple meals = more time for goals
- No one cares what your meal prep looks like except you
